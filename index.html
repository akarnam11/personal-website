About

Anirudh is an undergraduate student at the University of Michigan pursuing a Bachelor's of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Statistics. Anirudh is extremely passionate about his interests in backend engineering, machine learning, and artificial intelligence. Currently, Anirudh is working as a full-stack software engineer intern at Capital One, building an end-to-end testing dashboard for the Partnerships team within Card Tech. Before that, Anirudh worked as a back-end software engineer intern at Hubspot on the Growth Monetization Team. Anirudh has previously worked as a research intern alongside Prof. Cristian Minoccheri to develop advanced computational methods to address complex problems in medicine and biology. Anirudh previously interned with Deloitte, where he performed cybersecurity and cyber risk research associated with various F500 companies. He also worked with United Way (Pennsylvania non-profit) to optimize bus scheduling routes to reduce delays and save overhead costs.

Books

Various books that I've read and would recommend to give a try! Includes a summary and link to buy the book.

The Clean Coder

Legendary software expert Robert C. Martin introduces the disciplines, techniques, tools, and practices of true software craftsmanship. This book is packed with practical advice–about everything from estimating and coding to refactoring and testing.

Purchase for Yourself

Wuthering Heights

An 1847 novel by Emily Brontë, initially published under her pen name Ellis Bell. It concerns two families of the landed gentry living on the West Yorkshire moors, the Earnshaws and the Lintons, and their turbulent relationships with the Earnshaws' foster son, Heathcliff.

Purchase for Yourself

The Big Sleep

The first of a series featuring detective Philip Marlowe, the story is noted for its complexity, with characters double-crossing one another and secrets being exposed throughout the narrative. The title is a euphemism for death; the final pages of the book refer to a rumination about "sleeping the big sleep".

Purchase for Yourself

When Breath Becomes Air

The story of Paul Kalanithi, a neurosurgeon and neuroscientist who was diagnosed with and died from cancer in his mid-thirties. This story details his extraordinary journey in search of the meaning of life in the face of death.

Purchase for Yourself

Thinking, Fast and Slow

The book's main thesis is a differentiation between two modes of thought: "System 1" is fast, instinctive and emotional; "System 2" is slower, more deliberative, and more logical. Kahneman shows that our brains are highly evolved to perform many tasks with great efficiency, but they are often ill-suited to accurately carry out other mental tasks; in fact, our thinking is riddled with behavioral fallacies.

Purchase for Yourself
